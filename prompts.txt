2. Build the initial website

Build a professional one-page website for a design agency called "Void Studio."

## What Void Studio does
Logo design, website design, and brand identity systems for companies.
They are selective — only take a handful of clients per quarter.
Premium positioning, not a volume shop.

## Brand personality
- Confident but not arrogant
- Direct and opinionated — they show you one direction, not forty
- Thoughtful — they ask uncomfortable questions before picking up a pencil
- The kind of studio that has strong opinions about type and space

## Design process — USE the frontend-design skill
1. Ground the concept in the name "Void" — the creative space before a
   brand is born. This is the conceptual anchor. Build the entire visual
   language from this idea.
2. Develop a unique dark palette — NOT the default black + neon green or
   black + red. Something more considered.
3. Choose a type pairing specific to this brief. Not interchangeable with
   any other agency site. Syne is a good candidate for display.
4. Define one signature design element that makes this site memorable
   and can be justified by the brief.
5. Review your concept against the three AI design clichés listed in the
   skill and revise anything that defaults to those patterns.
6. Only start coding after the concept is fully written out.

## What to avoid
- Numbered section markers (01, 02, 03) — these services are parallel,
  not a sequence
- Neon accent colors (acid green, vermilion) as the default dark-site move
- Particle effects, cursor trails, or scattered micro-animations
- Stock photo placeholders or image-dependent layouts
- Copy that sounds like marketing: "unleash," "transform," "elevate,"
  "seamless," "journey," "cutting-edge"
- Generic sans-serif headlines that look like every other SaaS site

## Tech stack
Pure HTML + CSS + Vanilla JS. No framework, no build step.
Google Fonts via <link rel="preconnect">. Mobile-first responsive.
Respect prefers-reduced-motion throughout. Three files: index.html,
style.css, script.js.

## Sections (in order)

**Nav** — sticky, transparent → blur + dark on scroll.
"VOID STUDIO" wordmark left (Syne, small tracking).
Work / Services / About / [Start a Project] right.
CTA is a white pill button with void-colored text.

**Hero** — full viewport. This is where the signature design moment
lives. The concept: before any brand exists, there is a void. Make
that felt in the design itself.

**Ticker** — thin horizontal marquee strip:
LOGO DESIGN — BRAND IDENTITY — WEB DESIGN — VISUAL SYSTEMS — ART DIRECTION

**Selected Work** — 6 cards, 3-column grid. Use these projects exactly:
- Meri · Fintech Rebrand · 2025
- Kairos · Product Launch Identity · 2025
- Sable · Luxury Food Brand · 2024
- North Quarter · Real Estate Identity · 2024
- Ardent · SaaS Rebrand · 2024
- Fenix · App Icon & Brand System · 2023
Cards must feel atmospheric, not like empty placeholders. Use subtle
color variation to give each card a distinct identity without images.

**Services** — 3 columns side by side: Logo, Web, Brand.
Each has: a large display name, one sharp one-line tagline, 2-3 direct
sentences of description.
- Logo: "A mark that holds at 16px and on a billboard."
- Web: "Sites that load fast, feel considered, and convert."
- Brand: "The system that makes everything else make sense."

**About** — Asymmetric two-column layout.
Left: large italic pull quote — "Great brands don't describe themselves.
They appear."
Right: eyebrow label + 2 paragraphs. Voice: direct, confident, slightly
uncomfortable. "We ask uncomfortable questions, present one direction at
a time, and finish what we start. If you're looking for a studio that
shows you forty options, we're not it."

**Contact** — Large display headline: "Ready to exist?"
Sub: "We take on a small number of clients each quarter. If you're
building something worth believing in, let's talk."
Email link: hello@voidstudio.co

**Footer** — VOID STUDIO · © 2026 · Email · Instagram · LinkedIn

## Copy voice
Short sentences. Active verbs. Zero filler. No adjective unless it earns
its place. The studio speaks like it designs — with intention.

4. Refine the layout and centering

The hero card feels too wide and the heading text is too large — the
WebGL shader background is barely visible on the sides.

Make these specific changes:

1. Hero card: reduce from max-w-3xl (768px) to max-w-[520px] so the
   shader is clearly visible and atmospheric on both sides.

2. Hero heading font: reduce from clamp(2.5rem,8vw,6rem) to
   clamp(2rem,5vw,3.25rem) so the text reads in 3 compact lines
   rather than filling the entire card.

3. Nav: wrap the logo and links in an inner div with the same
   max-width as the page content (1200px, centered) so the nav
   aligns with sections below rather than spanning full viewport edges.

4. All section content: verify it uses a consistent centered container
   (max-width 1200px, auto margins, clamp horizontal padding).

The goal: the card is a small, focused window in the center of the void
with the chromatic wave clearly framing it on both sides.
